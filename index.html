Africa in the process of Peace

1Frederik De Klerk

He was a South African politician, president from 1989-1994. His presidency was characterized by a policy of reforms aimed at overcoming racial segregation of apartheid. To achieve this, he repealed existing segregationist laws, released several black politicians from prison, including Nelson Mandela, legalized the African National Congress (ANC), and endowed the country with a new non-racial constitution. However, economic reforms continued to benefit the white population, which slowed down the overcoming of racial segregation.

2Nelson Mandela (Madiba)

A South African leader, an icon of the fight against apartheid. In 1952, Mandela played a significant role in a campaign that challenged the so-called pass laws, which required black individuals to carry documents proving they were authorized to be in areas reserved for whites. Disagreeing with this, Mandela began his political involvement against apartheid.
