Peace Leaders.

Frederik De Klerk


He was a South African politician, president from 1989-1994. His presidency was characterized by a policy of reforms aimed at overcoming racial segregation of apartheid. To achieve this, he repealed existing segregationist laws, released several black politicians from prison, including Nelson Mandela, legalized the African National Congress (ANC), and endowed the country with a new non-racial constitution.

Nelson Mandela (Madiba)


A South African leader, an icon of the fight against apartheid. In 1952, Mandela played a significant role in a campaign that challenged the so-called pass laws, which required black individuals to carry documents proving they were authorized to be in areas reserved for whites. Disagreeing with this, Mandela began his political involvement against apartheid.

Frederik de Klerk


"He played an essential role in South Africa by leading political reforms that led to the transition to democracy and the end of apartheid. He released Nelson Mandela and lifted anti-apartheid bans, initiated historic dialogues with the African National Congress (ANC), and collaborated with the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC) to investigate apartheid crimes. His leadership enabled a peaceful transition to democracy in 1994, with elections that brought Mandela to the presidency. De Klerk was instrumental in leading political reforms, negotiating with the ANC, participating in the TRC, and in the transition towards peace and reconciliation in South Africa."

Key points:

1Points.

Racial segregation consisted of the systematic separation of different groups in daily life (cinemas, bathrooms, schools, etc.).

2Points.

Initially, violent forms of struggle were used, but in pursuit of lasting changes, non-violent forms were adopted.

3Points.

Apartheid (meaning "separation" in Afrikaans) was the system of racial segregation in South Africa and Namibia from 1948 to 1992. It involved injustices and violations of human rights.

4Points.

The Sharpeville massacre: A moment when police forces attacked people protesting against apartheid and the pass laws, setting them on fire and leaving around 69 dead and many injured.

5Points.

United Democratic Front (UDF): A coalition of over 700 civic, religious, student, and labor organizations whose goal was to establish a united South Africa.

Examples of peace points in their context that can be applied to Colombia

1. In South Africa, racial segregation consisted of the systematic separation of people in daily life activities, while in Colombia, discrimination against the Afro-Colombian population and others has been a triggering factor for violence in the central-western region of the country.
2. Initially, South Africa used violent forms of struggle, but in pursuit of lasting changes, nonviolent forms were adopted. In Colombia, there has been a period of violence lasting over 40 years that has directly or indirectly affected the entire population, still striving for an end to violence and the pursuit of peace.

3. Apartheid was the system of racial segregation in South Africa and Namibia from 1948 to 1992, involving injustices and human rights violations. Different insurgent groups in Colombia have been violating human rights since 1946.
4. The Sharpeville massacre was a moment when police forces attacked people protesting against apartheid and pass laws, setting them on fire and leaving around 69 dead and many injured. Various massacres throughout the Andean region of Colombia have sparked multiple protests in rejection of the violence.

5. United Democratic Front (UDF): a union of over 700 organizations aimed at establishing a unified South Africa.
6. Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC): In South Africa, the TRC played a crucial role in promoting healing and reconciliation by allowing victims and perpetrators of apartheid-era crimes to share their stories and seek amnesty. Implementing a similar mechanism in Colombia could provide a platform for addressing historical injustices, acknowledging victims' suffering, and fostering national unity through dialogue and accountability processes
